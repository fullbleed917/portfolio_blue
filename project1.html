Zushi Resturant, based in Mesa, Arizona has been in buisness for 7 years. A well established Sushi and Japanese Bistro that has recently expanded to offer Tappanyaki to there local customers.


As a result of their expansion and new remodel they were in need of an online prescense that accurrately portrays their brand, connects with customers and provides a better user experience. The new website was designed to incorporate modern features such as a online reservation system. A google map API location feature to better connect with clients trying to locate the resturant for the first time,be it for social gatherings or a happy hour meetup or buisness dinner with clients.

Previous Site Analysis

Client has an existing site that is that did not provide any real imapact on the buisness. There was no functionality. The User experience was very poor. There was no real brand strategy or effective communication with thes customers It was ineffective in three key areas. It failed to Improve Brand Awareness as there was little usable information and content

The site failed to build stronger customer relationships as there was no attempt to connect with the client base with social media, no testimonials or social proofing for potential clients. The images were not professional looking. pixellated and poor lighting and use of colors and the site failed to increase sales by not offering online booking of the Tappanyaki resturant

Wireframes and Prototypes

Given aspects as the content of the site, the business goals and several usabilty reviews wireframes were designed in Sketch for the user interaction and flow.

Visual Proposal

A high fidelity mockup was created in photoshop in order to acomplish the website objectives



OUTCOME & FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS

The redesign was well received within the company and much to our delight, the new design opened up the owner to expanding the scope of the build to a future iteration with a more indepth backend, performing better than the old one in key areas.

While this was a very basic application with a short timeline, I think for future considerations, it would have been benefical to spend more time on user testing by creating more interactive prototypes and running a few more variations.

As a whole very happy with how the site has come along and is moving towards hitting the customers goals and more.
